Purpose:

The Wellesley Hackathon (WHACK) is an annual hackathon event hosted at Wellesley where undergrads from all across Boston come together to build things they could only dream of! My role on the Web Development Team of the organizing committee was to help develop a site where students could gain vital information for all things WHACK. My job was to help the hackers hack as best as they can, providing links for any technical educational resources, the schedule of events, and contact information for mentors.

As a Web Development Team Member, I wanted to serve as the first source of creative inspiration, turning the beautiful branding and designs from the D&M team into a tangible interface that the 100+ hackers could refer to in need. I believe one of the best feelings in the world is seeing people let their imaginations run wild, and helping those dreams become realized. Being a part of WHACK let me do that.

Process:

During the first few weeks of the project, once we received the initial Figma prototype from the Design and Marketing team, the team got to implementing. After an initial meeting, the team lead and I decided to implement the design in a one-page website structure, using Bootstrap as a CSS framework.
